Food Safety Practices

CLEAN your hands, utensils and kitchen



· Always wash your hands using soap and running water and dry thoroughly on a clean towel.
· Use different tea towels to dry your hands and your dishes.
· Make sure your cooking utensils, chopping boards and kitchen bench tops are clean.
· Use multiple chopping boards for chopping different foods and wash boards using hot soapy water.
· Wash fruits and vegetables before eating.

COOK food properly


Cook foods thoroughly and make sure foods such as meats and poultry are cooked until their core temperature reaches 75 °C.
· Always cook meat such as poultry, mince and sausages right through until all juices are clear.
· Reheat leftovers to steaming hot before eating.
· Always follow cooking instructions on packaged food.

CHILL food promptly


Put food that needs to be kept cold in the fridge straight away and don't eat food that’s been left out for two hours or more.
· Keep your fridge below 5 °C.
· Return salads to the fridge promptly to keep them safe, fresh and tasty.
· Defrost and marinate foods in the fridge, especially meats.
· Shop with a cooler bag and picnic with an Esky.

SEPARATE raw and cooked foods


· Avoid cross-contamination by keeping raw foods and ready-to-eat foods separate.
· Always use separate, clean utensils, containers and equipment.
· Keep raw meat and poultry from touching other food and store in the bottom of the fridge, or in a sealed container.
· Cover all stored food.
